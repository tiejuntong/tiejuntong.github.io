      Frequently Asked Questions



      Question 1: What is the purpose to launch The B5 Club?

      Answer:
      As is well known, the top 4 journals in statistics consist of AoS, Biometrika, JASA, and JRSSB. For short, I now refer to them as The T4 Club. These 4 journals mainly focus on the statistical theory and methodology, and they are also, more or less, of the same style. In other words, if a statistician is extremely well trained in statistical theory so that s/he is able to independently publish papers in one of these journals, then assuming that s/he is also very productive, it will be very likely that this statistician will eventually join The T4 Club.

      While for The B5 Club, its 5 journals cover not only "all aspects ranging from probability, statistics to biostatistics", but also "all aspects ranging from theory, methodology to application". To conclude, The T4 Club can be used to represent the depth of statistical knowledge, whereas The B5 Club can be used to represent the breadth of statistical knowledge. In addition, noting that Biometrika belongs to both clubs, can we simply conclude that the statistical knowledge of the B5 club members is with only the breadth but not the depth?


      Question 2: Well, you almost convinced us. Is there anything else you would like to add about The B5 Club?

      Answer:
      As an additional message to share, the letter B in The B5 Club may also indicate the breadth of statistical knowledge, whereas the number 5 may imply that the B5 club members behave, in some sense, like pentagon warriors.


          



      Question 3: Besides The B5 Club and The T4 Club, are there other interesting clubs that can be attractive for grouping the statistics and related journals?

      Answer:
      This is indeed a very good question. At your own interest, you are most welcome to group a few top-tier journals in your field and launch your own clubs. Hopefully, of course, you will be a member of your own club, as otherwise what is the purpose to spend a lot of effort to launch such an irrelevant club, right? With a brainstorming discussion, the people in the Sai Kung trip (see above) have actually suggested a few other club names including, but not limited to,
      (a) The A3 Club (Annals of Mathematics, Annals of Probability, and Annals of Statistics) with, e.g., Terence Tao and Amir Dembo being two club members.
      (b) The A4 Club (Annals of Applied Probability, Annals of Probability, Annals of Applied Statistics, and Annals of Statistics) with, e.g., Persi Diaconis and David Siegmund being two club members.


      Question 4: Why is it you, but not others, have launched The B5 Club?

      Answer:
      Following the answer to Question 3, I do not expect that non-club members would be interested in launching such a club. While for most existing members in The B5 Club (expect for me, who does research mainly for fun), they have devoted themselves to cutting-edge research and will certainly not waste their precious time building and maintaining such a nonsense club. In the end, it is either me or nobody, who might be interested to launch this purely-for-fun club.
